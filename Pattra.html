Project Pattra

Papers transcend from being a tool of recording human thoughts, towards forming the core essence of our history. From recording the teachings of ancient philosophers such as Aristotle and Plato towards the framing of our Indian Constitution, papers have framed and influenced the lives of humankind. Their origins can be traced back to the Eastern Han period in China during 20-220 CE. Most of the papers available in the marketplace continue to remain wood-based. The industrial papermaking process continues to be of concern from an environmental perspective, due to the use of harsh chemicals, the need for large amounts of water, and the carbon sequestration lost by deforestation. This is caused by clearcutting the trees used as the primary source of wood pulp. Therefore the necessity of paper recycling becomes more eminent to ensure its sustenance in our lives.

Project Pattra is introduced to counterpoise the decreasing forest covers and resulting global warming. It aims to recycle paper waste into creative products of everyday use such as journals, bookmarks, paper bags, and much more. The etymology of the word Pattra in the project name can be traced back to the Aryans of the Vedic Period. Pattra refers to paper in Sanskrit. This initiative has helped in the creation of employment opportunities for "Thozhi".

Thozhi is a nonprofit community-based organization working for the welfare of the transgender community located in Chetpet, Chennai. It aims to bring forth gender equality for the transgender community. It raises awareness of issues faced by the Transgender community through education and training and strives to reduce discrimination and abuse through improved legislation and acceptance. Thozhi is a cut above the other organizations from being a shelter home towards enhancing mental health as well. It aims to create employment opportunities in all fields for transwomen. The organization conducts gender sensitizing seminars in workplaces to create awareness about an inclusive work environment.

The process of making recycled paper in Project Pattra is as follows. The raw material is mixed with water and beaten. Then the raw material is cut to make a pulp out of it. A washing drum is used to clean the pulp and drain the residual water. Sheets of handmade paper can be made in two ways:
Dipping Method: This method is normally used for papers with a low thickness (thin papers). The pulp is diluted with water and put into a masonry trough or vat. The lifting mold (a mesh on a wooden frame) is dipped into the trough, shaken evenly, and lifted out with the pulp on it. The consistency of the pulp in the tank should be kept constant all the time.

Lifting Method: This method is used for general-purpose papers (especially for cards). A fixed quantity of pulp is poured evenly onto a mold, which is then clamped between two wooden deckles (frames) and then dipped into a water tank. The mold is then lifted using a lever mechanism that allows the excess water to drain away.
Couching: Once the sheet is formed, the wet paper is transferred onto a cloth-like muslin or felt sheet, and a stack of interleaved sheets is made. The sheets are hung in the sunlight to dry. Solar dries can speed up the process and the space required for drying. Colored papers are dried in the shade to prevent the sun from bleaching the color.

This project encompasses societal well-being and sustainable utilization of natural resources. We hope to achieve self-sustenance and provide an alternate means of livelihood for our workforce. With a passionate and vibrant team, we envisage a better world for our workforce. By instilling new lives to used newspapers, magazines, and paper scraps we ensure sustenance for the environmental resources and provide a cleaner Earth for posterity.

Our Workforce

Project Pattra capacitates upskilling and provides entrepreneurial opportunities to our workforce - the transgender community at Thozhi. 'Thozhi' which stands for Transgender Health-Oriented Zealous Holistic Initiative is an organization situated in Chetpet that works for the welfare of transgenders. It is run for the transgenders by the transgenders, striving to make a change in the society. Training and employing a marginalised community for such an enterprise would empower them to become confident, independent individuals and help shed light on queer rights. Through their work, they can convey their words to the world. With collaboration and common motivation Project Pattra aims to build a more inclusive society.
